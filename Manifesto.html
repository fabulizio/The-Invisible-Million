The Collective

Who are we, when we are no one? We call ourselves a collective. But not in the traditional sense. There is no shared studio. No list of names. No centralized leadership. No artist statements signed in cursive. No mythology of genius. We are not here to be known. We are here to create a system where knowing is irrelevant. We are a faceless collective. No signatures. No glory. Just an idea: to build a digital artwork made of one million testimonies, each contributed by a different person. Each contribution is anonymous, unowned, irreproducible, (in)visible, and forever.

Who runs this?

Someone sets up the infrastructure. Someone maintains the archive. Someone pays for the server, the domain, the automation. But these roles are functional, not symbolic. We are not interested in credit, only in continuity. The invisible needs maintenance. Thatâ€™s the paradox. We accept it.

Philosophical Position

Behind this construction lies a precise philosophical stance: In an era obsessed with visibility, we believe that voluntary invisibility is a radical act. In a time when every action is tracked, posted, shown, we ask: What happens when the gesture detaches from the ego? What happens when you cannot trace a contribution back to its author? We offer no answers. We offer a system where that question remains alive.

Participation

Participation requires a minimum donation (1 euro or more). Not to sell art, but to create an intention filter. Nothing is free: the pixel is an act, not a product.

The Result

The final result is a public archive, permanently online, freely reusable by anyone. It can be explored, printed, analyzed. No one owns anything. No one will be thanked. But each person counts as one, and no more than one.
